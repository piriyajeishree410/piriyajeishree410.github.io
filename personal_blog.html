Miles into Boston, from boarding pass to growth

Hello Boston: First Glances

When I first glimpsed Boston through the airport windows, the city was wrapped in a blanket of snow. Coming from the warmth of India to the bite of –15°C, it felt like stepping into a snow-globe fairytale.

Every street, every rooftop, glistened with a beauty both magical and overwhelming, and in that moment, I knew I was far from home yet on the brink of a new chapter.

Campus Chronicles: Life between lectures 🤯

The shift from the Indian to the American education system was nothing short of transformative. It stretched me beyond my comfort zone, challenged the way I thought, and sometimes left me with imposter syndrome.

Long nights in the library that blurred into mornings, assignments that felt endless, and exams that demanded everything - it was a marathon. Yet, through the exhaustion came resilience, fresh perspectives, and the kind of growth only such intensity can bring.

City Stroll: Exploring one street at a time!

Though I’ve always been more of a couch-dweller than an adventurer, Boston coaxed me outdoors. My first true wanderings were during Christmas and New Year’s, when the city glittered with lights and joy.

Other times, I found peace by the Charles River, where even a busy city seemed to pause. I discovered grocery stores, tucked-away restaurants, and even braved my first nightclub — though dancing never quite felt like my rhythm. Boston, with its mix of history and modern vibrance, revealed itself in pieces, each one stitched into my memory.

The Inner Journey: Both Solitude and Longing...

This journey has turned me inward. I became quieter, more reserved, speaking mostly with my parents and close friends back home, while my social circle here shrank to a dot. Yet, in that solitude, I matured. I learned responsibility — that no one else will clean up my mess, that even when I stumble (literally down the stairs), I must care for myself.

The struggles, big and small, reshaped me into someone more independent, grounded, and self-reliant. Now I cook meals that comfort me, follow routines that steady me, and carry a strength I never knew I had.

SEO Best Practices

For optimal Search Engine Optimization, these HTML header tags are most important:

  • <title> - Page title shown in search results
  • <meta name="description"> - Page summary for search results
  • <h1> - Main page heading, should include primary keywords
  • <h2> - Section headings, should include secondary keywords
  • <meta name="keywords"> - Relevant keywords for the page
  • <header> - Semantic tag that helps search engines understand structure

10 Most Useful HTML Tags

  1. <div> - Block-level container for layout and styling

  3. <a> - Creates hyperlinks for navigation
  4. <img> - Embeds images into web pages
  5. <ul> / <ol> / <li> - Creates lists for content organization
